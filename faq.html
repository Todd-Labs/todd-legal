Everything people ask Todd, answered in full.

How does Todd log my workouts?

You talk (or type) like a human. Tap the mic and say something like "bench press, 3 sets of 10 at 185 with 2 minutes rest, then ran 2 miles in 17 minutes." Todd's AI parses that into a clean, structured log — each exercise gets its own card with sets, reps, weight, rest, duration, distance, pace, and steps, formatted for its type.

You never fill out a form. If Todd gets something wrong, tell him what to fix — "make set 2 six reps" — and he amends the entry.

What AI does Todd use?

Todd formats your entries with leading third-party AI models — primarily OpenAI's, with Anthropic's Claude models as a fallback. Requests travel through a secure server proxy, so no AI keys ever live on your phone.

Voice is transcribed by Deepgram, with Apple's on-device Speech framework as a backup. Text you submit through these services is not used to train the providers' models.

Is my data private and secure?

Your workouts, goals, and profile are stored locally on your device using Apple's SwiftData. Todd doesn't keep your fitness history on a server.

When you log or edit with AI, only the text or audio needed for that request is transmitted. For diagnostics, quality, and abuse prevention, requests and AI responses may be retained in our secure backend, associated with a randomly generated device identifier — not your name. Nothing is used for advertising. The full details live in the Privacy Policy.

How does the free trial work?

It's not a ticking clock — Todd is free to try for a limited number of workout logs. Log your first workouts completely free and see how the bookkeeping feels. When you're ready to keep logging, pick a plan. Everything you logged stays on your device either way.

How do I cancel my subscription?

Subscriptions are billed and managed entirely by Apple:

  • Open Settings on your iPhone
  • Tap your name → Subscriptions
  • Select Todd → Cancel Subscription

Cancel at least 24 hours before your renewal date and you won't be charged again. Refund requests are handled by Apple.

What happens to my data if I cancel?

Your books stay yours. Every workout, goal, and trend you've logged remains on your device, fully viewable. Logging new workouts with Todd's AI requires an active subscription — but Todd never holds your history hostage.

If you want your backend diagnostic data deleted too, email toddlabs413@gmail.com and we'll take care of it.

Can Todd make mistakes?

Occasionally — he's an accountant, not a psychic. AI can misinterpret an unusual phrasing, and slang like "2 plates" is deliberately kept as-is rather than converted into a guessed weight. Everything is editable: tell Todd the correction by voice, or edit any entry by hand.

Also worth stating plainly: Todd is a workout formatting and tracking tool. He does not provide medical advice, personalized training programs, or healthcare recommendations. Estimated one-rep maxes are theoretical math (the Epley formula) — never attempt a max lift based solely on an estimate.

What types of workouts can Todd log?

Around twenty categories, each with its own tailored layout:

  • Strength — weightlifting, bodyweight, supersets, warmups, drop sets
  • Cardio — running, swimming, cycling, rowing, sprints, with pace and splits
  • Walking & steps — "walked 10,000 steps today" is a real, tracked entry
  • Machine cardio — treadmill, elliptical, StairMaster, with incline, level, and resistance
  • Everything else — yoga (pose by pose), HIIT, Tabata, boxing, circuits, climbing, jump rope, sports, stretching, dance, and more

What kinds of goals can Todd track?

Goals Todd can measure from your logs: strength targets (a 225 bench), rep goals (100 push-ups), timed holds (3-minute plank), speed goals (a sub-5 mile or a 40-yard dash), consistency (4 workouts a week), volume, and totals by a deadline — reps, sessions, distance, or steps.

Daily goals get special treatment: say "100 push-ups a day for 30 days" and your goal card shows one circle per day — hit the day's number and it fills with a green check. The day you create the goal counts as day one.

Do I need an account?

No. There's no sign-up, no login, no password to forget. You open Todd, tell him your name, and start logging. Your records belong to your device — not to an account on someone's server.

Does Todd sync between devices?

Not currently — your data lives on the device where you logged it, which is part of why it stays private. If you switch phones, your logs don't follow automatically. Cross-device sync is something we're considering for the future.

Does Todd work offline?

Viewing your logs, goals, and trends works offline — the data is on your device. Logging or editing with AI needs an internet connection, since the formatting happens on secure servers.

I forgot to log a workout. Can I backdate it?

Yes — open the Calendar tab, pick the day, and log it by voice or keyboard just like normal. Backdated workouts count toward goals whose window covers that day, including filling in a missed circle on a daily goal.

Why an accountant?

Because your gains deserve honest bookkeeping. Todd doesn't cheer indiscriminately or invent numbers — he records exactly what you tell him, shows you the trends, and raises an eyebrow when the books don't balance. Show up, and he's genuinely proud. The books don't lie.
